Type your address into any real estate site and you'll get an instant number. It's tempting to treat that as your home's value — but those estimates are often off by a wide margin, and trusting one can cost you real money. Here's how home valuation actually works in Southeast Wisconsin.

## Why online estimates miss

Automated estimates are generated by algorithms that crunch public records and past sales. They're a useful starting point, but they have real blind spots. They can't see inside your home. They don't know you renovated the kitchen, finished the basement, or that the photos online are ten years out of date. They also struggle in neighborhoods with few recent sales or lots of variety between homes — which describes a lot of Southeast Wisconsin. The result is a number that can be thousands of dollars too high or too low.

Use them for a rough ballpark. Don't price your home on one.

## What actually determines value

A home's market value comes down to what a willing buyer will pay in current conditions. Several factors drive that:

- **Location** — the neighborhood, school district, and even the specific street. This is the biggest single factor and the one you can't change.
- **Comparable sales** — what similar nearby homes have actually sold for recently. This is the backbone of any serious valuation.
- **Condition and updates** — a move-in-ready home commands more than one needing work. Kitchens, bathrooms, roof, and mechanicals carry weight.
- **Size and layout** — square footage, bedroom and bathroom count, and how usable the space is.
- **Current market conditions** — how many homes are for sale and how many buyers are looking right now.
- **Wisconsin-specific factors** — basement condition, furnace and roof age, and energy efficiency matter to buyers who know what our winters demand.

## The comparative market analysis

The tool professionals actually use is a comparative market analysis, or CMA. An agent pulls recent sales of genuinely comparable homes nearby — similar size, condition, and location — and adjusts for the differences to arrive at a realistic range for your home. Unlike an algorithm, a good agent can account for the things only a person who's walked your street would know.

## Why getting the price right matters so much

Pricing isn't just about knowing a number — it's strategy. Price too high and your listing lingers, loses momentum, and often sells for *less* than if it had been priced correctly from day one, because buyers grow suspicious of homes that sit. Price it well and you can generate strong interest and, sometimes, competing offers. The price you set in week one shapes everything that follows.
